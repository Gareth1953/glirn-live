# GLIRN Mission 40 To 47 Summary

## Overview

Missions 40 to 47 transformed GLIRN from a set of specialist legal recruitment engines into a coordinated executive operating system.

The platform remains read-only, human-controlled, and non-autonomous.

Current test status: 198 passed.

## Mission 40: Executive Autopilot

Business purpose: Give Gareth a single recommendation layer instead of requiring manual review across every GLIRN section.

Technical purpose: Aggregate Opportunity Radar, Executive Search, Client Acquisition, Candidate Discovery, Matching, Commercial Revenue, and Compliance Core.

Output:

- Top opportunity.
- Top candidate.
- Top client.
- Top placement match.
- Highest estimated fee.
- Highest placement probability.
- Compliance alerts.
- Gareth approval queue.

Safety:

- No autonomous outreach.
- No autonomous introduction.
- No autonomous fee proposal.

## Mission 41: Live Data Readiness

Business purpose: Prepare for future real-world data without connecting to anything yet.

Technical purpose: Define source registry, readiness scoring, and source approval controls.

Output:

- Source registry.
- Source readiness summary.
- Blocked sources.
- Approved sources.
- Pending sources.
- Dave Recommends First.

Safety:

- No scraping.
- No live fetching.
- No candidate data ingestion.
- High-risk sources blocked by default.

## Mission 42: Integration Governance

Business purpose: Stop future integrations being added informally or unsafely.

Technical purpose: Create a governance framework for proposed, approved, blocked, and suspended integrations.

Output:

- Integration registry.
- Compliance score.
- Approval score.
- Readiness score.
- Risk score.
- Governance status.
- Governance alerts.

Safety:

- No live integrations.
- No outbound connections.
- No autonomous activation.

## Mission 43: Deployment Readiness

Business purpose: Make launch readiness explicit before GLIRN is used with real-world data or clients.

Technical purpose: Score technical, compliance, commercial, operational, documentation, and integration readiness.

Output:

- Readiness grade.
- Critical gaps.
- Recommended actions.
- Launch checklist.

Safety:

- Assessment only.
- No deployment action.

## Result

GLIRN now has a layered executive operating model:

1. Specialist engines.
2. Executive Autopilot.
3. Operations Command Centre.
4. Daily Executive Briefing.
5. Deployment Readiness controls.
6. Source and integration governance.

The platform is stronger internally but still not ready for real-world launch until data, consent, client terms, backup, hosting, and legal wording are reviewed.
