# GLIRN Fee Model

## Identity

Public brand: David Sanson

Business name: Global Legal Intelligence & Recruitment Network

Legal and tax owner: Gareth Price

Positioning: Human-led, technology-enhanced, Gareth-approved.

## Revenue Model Overview

GLIRN is designed around legal intelligence as the client hook and recruitment placements as the main revenue engine.

Primary revenue comes from successful or retained legal recruitment work. Secondary revenue may come from intelligence reports or subscriptions where appropriate.

## Primary Fee Types

### Contingency Placement Fee

Used where the client pays only after a successful placement.

Typical basis:

- Percentage of candidate first-year compensation.
- Suitable for associate, senior associate, legal director, and some in-house roles.

### Executive Search Fee

Used for senior, high-value mandates.

Typical roles:

- Partner.
- General Counsel.
- Chief Legal Officer.
- Legal Director.

Typical basis:

- Higher percentage of compensation due to seniority and complexity.

### Retained Search Fee

Used where the client commits to a structured search.

Typical basis:

- Staged retainer.
- Retainer credited against expected placement fee where commercially appropriate.

## Secondary Fee Types

### Legal Intelligence Report Fee

Used where a client wants a market signal report before starting a search.

Examples:

- Practice area demand report.
- Jurisdiction demand report.
- Salary pressure report.
- Hiring trend report.

### Subscription Intelligence Fee

Used where a client wants recurring legal market intelligence.

Examples:

- Monthly practice area intelligence.
- Quarterly jurisdiction hiring report.
- Senior legal talent market watch.

## Fee Controls

No fee proposal should be issued without Gareth Price approval.

GLIRN should check:

- Client terms status.
- Candidate consent status where candidate-specific.
- Estimated placement value.
- Fee type.
- Invoice readiness.
- Compliance readiness.
- Audit record.

## Indicative Commercial Priorities

Highest priority:

- Partner and executive search.
- General Counsel and Chief Legal Officer mandates.
- High-value practice areas such as Private Equity, Corporate & M&A, Banking & Finance, Technology & AI Law, Energy & Infrastructure.

Medium priority:

- Legal Director.
- Senior Associate.
- Specialist commercial legal hires.

Lower priority:

- Low-fee roles.
- Unclear client terms.
- Missing consent.
- Weak evidence.

## Human Approval

Gareth Price approval is mandatory before:

- Fee discussion.
- Fee proposal.
- Retainer proposal.
- Candidate submission.
- Invoice readiness progression.

## Boundaries

Revenue estimates are not guarantees.

GLIRN does not:

- Move capital.
- Issue invoices autonomously.
- Negotiate fees autonomously.
- Send fee proposals autonomously.
- Execute placements without human approval.
