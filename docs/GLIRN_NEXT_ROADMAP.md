# GLIRN Next Roadmap

- Mission 113: Internal Learning System - completed, verified, and approved by Gareth.
- Mission 113A: External Legal Intelligence & Compliance Learning - completed, verified, and approved by Gareth.
- Mission 114: Opportunity Intelligence Engine - completed, verified, and approved by Gareth.
- Mission 115: Self-Learning Legal Recruitment Brain - completed, verified, and approved by Gareth.
- Growth Phase 1: Visibility Preparation Engine - completed, verified, and approved by Gareth. Publication preparation remains local and manual, with Gareth approval required.
- Growth Phase 1A: Complimentary Hiring Snapshot and premium offer hierarchy - completed, verified, and approved by Gareth. The £500 GLIRN Senior Legal Hiring Intelligence Review remains the visible deeper paid offer.
- Growth Phase 1B: Controlled 25-Firm Introduction Mailer - completed, verified, and approved by Gareth. Campaign preparation, approval, suppression, and explicit manual sending remain separate governed actions.

Mission 113 and Mission 113A remain recommendation-only systems. Gareth approval is mandatory for learning insight consideration and knowledge-base updates. No autonomous decision, outreach, referral, payment, delivery, regulatory change, or external commitment is enabled.

## Priority Order

The next roadmap should harden GLIRN before adding live external integrations. Priority should remain compliance, human approval, data quality, revenue validation, and pilot readiness.

## Mission 40: Release Hardening and Documentation QA

Business purpose: Make GLIRN understandable, reviewable, and stable enough for Gareth to assess as a serious commercial platform.

Technical purpose: Review dashboard payloads, docs, API response consistency, action locks, and audit events.

Revenue relevance: Reduces confusion and increases readiness for commercial packaging.

Priority: 1

## Mission 41: Human-Approved Data Intake Specification

Business purpose: Define how Gareth can load candidate, client, and opportunity data without scraping or private-data-heavy automation.

Technical purpose: Specify CSV and manual intake formats for candidates, clients, consent records, terms records, and recruitment opportunities.

Revenue relevance: Converts GLIRN from stub-only data to controlled real-world usage.

Priority: 2

## Mission 42: Consent and Terms Evidence Pack

Business purpose: Strengthen trust by showing exactly why a candidate or client action is allowed or blocked.

Technical purpose: Add documentation and record structure for consent evidence, terms evidence, expiry dates, deletion status, and audit references.

Revenue relevance: Supports higher-value recruitment workflows where compliance confidence matters.

Priority: 3

## Mission 43: GLIRN Pilot Simulation

Business purpose: Prove the workflow using one simulated candidate, one simulated client, and one simulated placement match.

Technical purpose: Run an end-to-end dry run across opportunity radar, compliance, approval, candidate discovery, client acquisition, matching, and commercial revenue.

Revenue relevance: Demonstrates whether the system can support a real placement process.

Priority: 4

## Mission 44: Candidate and Client Record Quality Scoring

Business purpose: Help Gareth avoid weak, incomplete, or risky records.

Technical purpose: Add quality scoring for missing fields, stale consent, weak terms, incomplete compensation data, and poor evidence quality.

Revenue relevance: Improves match quality and reduces wasted effort.

Priority: 5

## Mission 45: Human Approval Evidence Bundle

Business purpose: Make every Gareth approval easier by collecting the relevant facts in one review bundle.

Technical purpose: Create a structured approval summary including opportunity, candidate, client, compliance, revenue, risk, and recommendation details.

Revenue relevance: Reduces time to review and improves confidence before commercial action.

Priority: 6

## Mission 46: Placement Readiness Checklist

Business purpose: Define when a placement opportunity is ready to progress.

Technical purpose: Create readiness checks for active consent, client terms, matching score, revenue score, approval state, audit trail, and missing evidence.

Revenue relevance: Focuses Gareth on the highest-probability fee opportunities.

Priority: 7

## Mission 47: GLIRN Revenue Forecast Report

Business purpose: Show Gareth the value of the current pipeline in plain commercial terms.

Technical purpose: Produce a report covering estimated fee pipeline, weighted revenue, highest-value matches, blocked revenue, and approval bottlenecks.

Revenue relevance: Directly links platform activity to potential revenue.

Priority: 8

## Mission 48: Candidate Privacy and Data Minimisation Review

Business purpose: Keep GLIRN credible and conservative around personal data.

Technical purpose: Review what candidate fields are necessary, what can be excluded, and what must remain locked unless consent is active.

Revenue relevance: Reduces compliance and trust barriers for future commercial use.

Priority: 9

## Mission 49: Client Intelligence Hook Packaging

Business purpose: Package non-personal legal market intelligence as a client hook before any recruitment discussion.

Technical purpose: Define report templates that do not expose candidate personal data and require Gareth approval before client-facing use.

Revenue relevance: Creates a softer entry point for client interest while preserving recruitment as the main revenue engine.

Priority: 10

## Mission 50: GLIRN V1 Pilot Readiness Review

Business purpose: Decide whether GLIRN is ready for a controlled pilot or should remain in internal simulation.

Technical purpose: Review tests, dashboards, audit events, data intake, consent evidence, approval bundles, and action locks.

Revenue relevance: Determines whether Gareth can safely test GLIRN with a narrow commercial workflow.

Priority: 11

## Roadmap Principles

- No autonomous candidate outreach.
- No autonomous client outreach.
- No candidate detail sharing without active consent and Gareth approval.
- No fee proposal without recorded client terms and Gareth approval.
- No scraping.
- No capital execution.
- No trading, crypto, gambling, medical advice, or regulated financial advice.
- Documentation, evidence, and auditability should come before automation.
