# GLIRN Controlled Launch Assessment

## Purpose

This document assesses whether the Global Legal Intelligence & Recruitment Network is ready to move from launch preparation into controlled launch activity.

Controlled launch means Gareth may use GLIRN-generated intelligence, rankings, reviews, deliverables, terms drafts, invoice drafts, consent records, and readiness checks to support one narrow first-client activity, but all external activity remains human-controlled and Gareth-approved.

## Controlled Launch Definition

GLIRN may:

- Analyse internal GLIRN data.
- Rank first prospect profiles.
- Generate draft intelligence reviews.
- Generate draft client deliverables.
- Generate draft client terms.
- Generate draft invoice records.
- Prepare candidate consent records.
- Prepare manual delivery packs.
- Validate launch compliance readiness.
- Recommend Gareth's next review action.
- Block unsafe or incomplete items.
- Record Gareth approval decisions.

Gareth must approve:

- Any use of a review with a prospect.
- Any use of a deliverable with a prospect.
- Any client conversation.
- Any client outreach.
- Any candidate activity.
- Any use of candidate-specific data.
- Any terms use.
- Any fee proposal.
- Any invoice use.
- Any manual delivery.

GLIRN must never do:

- Autonomous outreach.
- Autonomous client contact.
- Autonomous candidate contact.
- Autonomous delivery.
- Autonomous candidate introduction.
- Autonomous fee proposal.
- Autonomous contract acceptance.
- Autonomous invoicing.
- Automatic payment collection.
- Automatic payment confirmation.
- External integrations.
- Scraping.
- Live data fetching.
- Legal advice.
- Legal certification.
- Global legal compliance declaration.

## Recommended Controlled Launch Path

1. Use First Prospect Selection Engine to choose one prospect profile.
2. Generate one GLIRN Senior Legal Hiring Intelligence Review.
3. Generate one relevant client deliverable.
4. Generate client terms draft.
5. Generate invoice draft if a paid review is intended.
6. Confirm candidate consent is not required, or verify active consent if candidate-specific data is included.
7. Prepare manual delivery pack.
8. Run Launch Compliance Validation Engine.
9. Gareth reviews the complete pack.
10. Gareth approves, rejects, or monitors.
11. If approved, Gareth manually performs the external action.

## Go / No-Go Assessment

GO for internal controlled launch rehearsal:

- Yes.
- GLIRN can support a complete dry run without external contact.

GO for one human-controlled first-client action:

- Conditional.
- Requires Gareth approval after the dry run and Launch Compliance Validation Engine review.

NO-GO for autonomous launch:

- Mandatory.
- GLIRN must not launch, contact, send, invoice, collect payment, or engage externally by itself.

## Final Recommendation

Remain in launch preparation until one full dry run is completed.

After a clean dry run, GLIRN may move into controlled launch for one narrow first-client activity, provided Gareth manually approves and performs the action.
