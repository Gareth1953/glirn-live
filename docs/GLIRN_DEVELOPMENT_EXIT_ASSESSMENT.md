# GLIRN Development Exit Assessment

## Assessment Purpose

This document assesses whether the Global Legal Intelligence & Recruitment Network can formally exit development mode and enter controlled launch preparation.

This is not approval for real-world client activity. It is an assessment of whether the platform has enough structure, controls, documentation, and safety gating to move from product development into launch preparation.

## Current Platform Position

Product name: Global Legal Intelligence & Recruitment Network

Public-facing brand: David Sanson

Legal and tax owner: Gareth Price

Operating model: Human-led, technology-enhanced, Gareth-approved.

Current mission count: 60

Current test count: 247 passed

Current status: mature internal platform with launch readiness controls, not yet externally launched.

## 1. Technical Readiness

Assessment: strong for internal controlled launch preparation.

Evidence:

- Current automated test status: 247 passed.
- `/health` has previously verified healthy during recent mission checkpoints.
- Dashboard and UI include the main GLIRN control panels.
- Readiness, revenue, deliverable, approval, and launch layers are integrated into `/glirn/dashboard`.
- Audit logging exists for approval actions.
- Human approval endpoints exist.
- No external integrations are required for current operation.

Current technical strengths:

- Modular GLIRN engines.
- Clear read-only assessment layers.
- Existing test coverage across GLIRN functionality.
- Controlled endpoint actions with audit logging.
- Explicit safety flags in responses.

Technical limitations:

- Data remains stub/internal.
- No production deployment environment has been validated.
- No real external data source has been approved.
- No production authentication model has been reviewed for external use.
- No production backup/restore exercise has been completed.

Technical readiness conclusion: sufficient to exit development mode and enter controlled launch preparation. Not sufficient for autonomous production operation.

Compliance readiness conclusion: ready for controlled launch preparation only. Real-world activity should remain blocked until client terms, consent process, and compliance review position are confirmed.

## 8. Recommended Launch Phase

Recommended phase: enter launch preparation.

Reason:

GLIRN has enough internal capability, documentation, safety gating, readiness scoring, and operating procedure to leave pure development mode. However, it has not yet completed the real-world prerequisites needed for controlled launch.

Launch preparation should focus on:

- Confirming terms.
- Confirming payment.
- Selecting one prospect.
- Preparing one human-approved review.
- Testing one manual, non-automated client interaction.

## 9. Recommended First Real-World Activity

Recommended first real-world activity:

Prepare one anonymised, non-candidate-specific senior legal hiring intelligence review for one clearly defined prospect profile, then ask Gareth to approve a manual human delivery step.

Constraints:

- No candidate personal data.
- No automated outreach.
- No automated delivery.
- No fee proposal unless Gareth approves.
- No invoice unless payment and terms process are confirmed.
- No external system integration.

Preferred first offer:

A fixed-scope senior legal hiring intelligence review, priced modestly, used to test whether a prospect values GLIRN's intelligence-led recruitment positioning.

## 10. Final Recommendation

Recommendation: enter launch preparation.

Do not remain in development only.

Do not enter controlled launch yet.

Rationale:

- The platform has passed 247 tests.
- Core GLIRN modules are implemented.
- Safety controls are explicit.
- Readiness controls are implemented.
- Operating procedure is documented.
- Revenue path is documented.
- Launch blockers are known.

Controlled launch should begin only after the critical blockers are cleared and Gareth approves one narrow first-client human action.
