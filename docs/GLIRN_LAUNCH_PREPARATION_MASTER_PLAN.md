# GLIRN Launch Preparation Master Plan

## Purpose

This master plan defines the exact activities required to move the Global Legal Intelligence & Recruitment Network from development complete to launch preparation, and then from launch preparation to controlled launch.

The plan is documentation only. It does not authorise outreach, delivery, website publishing, LinkedIn posting, fee proposals, candidate activity, client engagement, invoicing, external integrations, scraping, or live data fetching.

## 2. Remaining Launch Blockers

### Client Terms Process

Status: blocked until confirmed.

Why it matters:

Client terms are required before candidate details, search mandates, fee proposals, placement activity, or invoice readiness.

Required activities:

- Define standard client terms position.
- Define when terms must be recorded.
- Define how terms status is logged.
- Confirm Gareth approval rule for any client terms.
- Confirm no client-facing candidate activity occurs without recorded terms.

Completion condition:

Client terms process is documented, Gareth-approved, and ready to use manually.

### Payment Process

Status: blocked until confirmed.

Why it matters:

GLIRN cannot enter controlled commercial activity without a manual payment route for review fees, search fees, and eventual invoice handling.

Required activities:

- Confirm payment method for first GBP 500 engagement.
- Confirm how payment receipt is recorded.
- Confirm invoice readiness checklist.
- Confirm Gareth handles all payment and invoice steps manually.

Completion condition:

Payment process is documented and Gareth-approved.

### Manual Delivery Process

Status: blocked until confirmed.

Why it matters:

GLIRN generates drafts. It must not deliver anything. Gareth needs a manual process for using approved reviews or deliverables.

Required activities:

- Confirm where final human-reviewed materials are stored.
- Confirm how Gareth checks approval status before use.
- Confirm candidate personal data exclusion.
- Confirm no automated sending.

Completion condition:

Manual delivery process is documented and tested without contacting a real prospect.

### Compliance Validation

Status: blocked until reviewed.

Why it matters:

Legal recruitment involves consent, data handling, jurisdiction, candidate privacy, and client terms issues.

Required activities:

- Confirm compliance review position.
- Confirm jurisdiction check process.
- Confirm data retention position.
- Confirm candidate data exclusion rule for first intelligence review.

Completion condition:

Compliance position is reviewed and Gareth accepts the risk controls for a narrow first-client activity.

### Candidate Consent Validation

Status: blocked for candidate-specific activity.

Why it matters:

Candidate-specific external use requires active consent.

Required activities:

- Define consent validation process.
- Define consent expiry check.
- Define candidate detail sharing approval rule.
- Confirm first activity uses no candidate personal data unless consent is active.

Completion condition:

Candidate consent process is documented and Gareth-approved.

### First Prospect Selection

Status: not complete.

Why it matters:

Controlled launch needs one narrow, suitable first prospect rather than a broad campaign.

Required activities:

- Select one prospect profile.
- Confirm sector.
- Confirm jurisdiction.
- Confirm likely need.
- Confirm no private data dependency.
- Confirm readiness score.

Completion condition:

One first prospect profile is selected and approved for human review.

### First Approved Human Action

Status: not approved.

Why it matters:

No first-client activity may occur until Gareth approves a specific human action.

Required activities:

- Select one action.
- Confirm readiness gate status.
- Confirm launch readiness status.
- Record Gareth approval.
- Keep action manual.

Completion condition:

Gareth approves one narrow human-controlled action and the approval is recorded.

## 5. First Prospect Selection Checklist

### Ideal First Prospect

The ideal first prospect is:

- A small or mid-sized law firm, boutique legal practice, legal department, or legal-sector buyer.
- In a familiar or clearly defined jurisdiction.
- Likely to care about senior legal hiring intelligence.
- Suitable for an intelligence-led conversation.
- Not dependent on candidate personal data.
- Not dependent on scraping or private company data.

### Sector

Recommended initial sectors:

- Private Equity.
- Corporate & M&A.
- Technology & AI Law.
- In-House Counsel.
- Partner & Executive Search.

### Jurisdiction

Recommended initial jurisdiction:

- England & Wales, unless Gareth deliberately selects another jurisdiction after review.

### Firm Profile

Target profile:

- Law firm, boutique practice, or legal department with visible senior hiring pressure.
- Clear practice area focus.
- Possible need for partner, senior associate, legal director, or in-house counsel talent.
- Commercially capable of paying for intelligence or search support.

### Revenue Potential

Minimum target:

- First GBP 500 intelligence review fee.

Secondary target:

- Search mandate leading toward first placement fee.

### Readiness Criteria

The first prospect should pass:

- Client profile confirmed.
- Target sector confirmed.
- Jurisdiction confirmed.
- Offer confirmed.
- Candidate personal data excluded.
- Client terms process ready.
- Payment process ready.
- Manual delivery process ready.
- Gareth approval recorded.

## 7. First Client Conversation Checklist

### Preparation

- Confirm prospect profile.
- Confirm reason for conversation.
- Confirm review scope.
- Confirm what will not be discussed.
- Confirm no candidate names or personal data.
- Confirm Gareth approval.

### Discovery Questions

Use questions such as:

- What senior legal hiring pressure are you seeing?
- Which practice areas are hardest to hire for?
- Which jurisdictions matter most?
- Are delays in hiring creating commercial risk?
- Would a focused hiring intelligence review be useful?
- What would make a search mandate worthwhile?

### Intelligence Review Usage

- Use the review as a discussion aid.
- Do not present it as guaranteed market truth.
- Do not include candidate-specific data.
- Do not imply automatic candidate access.

### Search Discussion

Only discuss search if:

- The client has a real hiring need.
- Client terms process is ready.
- Fee model is approved.
- Gareth approves the discussion.

### Next Steps

Record:

- Client interest level.
- Questions asked.
- Objections.
- Whether paid review, monitoring, or search mandate is appropriate.
- Follow-up action requiring Gareth approval.
