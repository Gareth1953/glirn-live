# GLIRN Controlled Global Launch Playbook

## 1. Executive Summary

Internal GLIRN Engine status: V1 complete with autonomous internal operations active.

Global Public Identity status: Ready with conditions.

Controlled Global Operation purpose: move GLIRN from internal autonomous preparation into first real-world, human-approved commercial activity while preserving Gareth Price as final authority.

GLIRN remains internally autonomous.

Gareth remains final approval authority only.

GLIRN may internally analyse, rank, generate, prepare, validate, block unsafe items, and create final approval packages. GLIRN must not perform external activity autonomously.

Controlled Global Operation means:

- GLIRN prepares the work internally.
- Gareth approves or rejects the final package.
- David Sanson performs approved external activity manually.
- Gareth signs off billing, payment, and money movement.

## 2. Controlled Global Operating Model

### GLIRN Internally Performs

- Opportunity detection
- Prospect selection
- Revenue prioritisation
- Intelligence review generation
- Deliverable generation
- Terms drafting
- Invoice drafting
- Consent validation
- Compliance validation
- Delivery pack preparation
- Final approval package generation

### Gareth Performs Only

- Final approval
- Final rejection
- Billing sign-off
- Payment sign-off
- Money movement sign-off

### David Sanson Performs

- Approved external prospect engagement
- Approved client-facing communication
- Approved delivery of intelligence review or recruitment materials
- Approved follow-up with prospects or clients

David Sanson must act only after Gareth approval has been recorded.

## 3. Controlled Launch Sequence

### Stage 1: GLIRN Generates Approval Package

GLIRN runs the internal autonomous cycle:

1. Detect opportunity.
2. Select prospect profile.
3. Prioritise revenue route.
4. Generate intelligence review.
5. Generate deliverable.
6. Draft terms.
7. Draft invoice.
8. Validate consent readiness.
9. Validate compliance readiness.
10. Prepare manual delivery pack.
11. Generate final Gareth approval package.

Output:

- Final Gareth approval package.
- Recommendation: approve, reject, or monitor.
- Blockers and warnings.

### Stage 2: Gareth Approves Or Rejects

Gareth reviews:

- Recommended prospect profile
- Recommended offer
- Expected revenue
- Revenue route
- Intelligence review status
- Deliverable status
- Terms status
- Invoice status
- Consent status
- Compliance status
- Delivery pack status
- Dry run status
- Blockers
- Warnings
- Final recommendation

Possible decisions:

- Approve
- Reject
- Monitor

### Stage 3: Approved Package Moves To Manual Execution

Only Gareth-approved packages may move to manual execution.

Manual execution means the system does not send, contact, invoice, collect payment, or accept terms automatically.

### Stage 4: David Sanson Performs Approved External Activity

David Sanson may perform only the activity Gareth approved.

Examples:

- Send an approved prospect message.
- Deliver an approved intelligence review.
- Discuss an approved offer.
- Follow up on an approved client conversation.

No candidate details may be shared without active consent.

### Stage 5: Invoice Manually Issued

Invoice issuing is manual.

GLIRN may prepare the invoice draft, but David Sanson or Gareth must issue it manually after approval.

### Stage 6: Payment Received

Payment collection is manual.

Supported manual payment routes may include:

- PayPal Business
- Revolut UK Bank Transfer

No payment API integration is authorised.

### Stage 7: Revenue Event Recorded

Gareth confirms payment and records the revenue event.

GLIRN may use the revenue event for later learning and V2 planning, but it must not confirm payment automatically.

## 5. Definition Of First Revenue Event

GLIRN's first revenue event occurs when:

- A GLIRN-approved service is delivered.
- Invoice is manually issued.
- Payment is received.
- Gareth confirms payment.

Examples:

- GBP 500 Intelligence Review completed and paid.
- Placement fee received.
- Retained search payment received.
- Market intelligence service fee received.

The first revenue event is not:

- A generated review.
- A prospect selected by GLIRN.
- An approval package.
- A sent proposal without payment.
- An unpaid invoice.
- A verbal expression of interest.

## 7. Controlled Launch Failure Conditions

Failure conditions include:

- External activity attempted without Gareth approval.
- Compliance checks bypassed.
- Candidate data used without active consent.
- Client terms process ignored.
- Invoice issued without approval.
- Payment process fails.
- Manual execution fails.
- Prospect does not understand the offer.
- Prospect sees no value.
- Prospect would not pay.
- Public messaging overclaims capability.
- David Sanson public activity discloses internal autonomous operations.

Required actions after failure:

1. Pause launch activity.
2. Conduct review.
3. Identify root cause.
4. Update operating procedure if required.
5. Update public messaging if required.
6. Update GLIRN controls only if the failure exposes a genuine system gap.
7. Do not expand automation until the issue is resolved.

## 8. Post-Revenue Review

After the first revenue event, conduct:

- Lessons learned review
- Offer clarity review
- Prospect quality review
- Pricing review
- Manual execution review
- Billing and payment review
- Compliance and consent review
- Automation gap analysis
- V2 enhancement assessment

Key questions:

- Why did the client pay?
- Which part of the offer created value?
- What slowed down the sale?
- What was unnecessary?
- What should remain manual?
- What could be improved in V2?
- Did any safety or compliance control need strengthening?

## 9. Recommendation

Decision: Ready for Controlled Global Operation

Rationale:

GLIRN V1 is internally operational and can generate final Gareth approval packages. The public identity layer is ready with conditions. The remaining task is not more V1 development; it is controlled revenue validation through one human-approved activity.

Conditions:

- Gareth approval remains mandatory.
- David Sanson performs only approved external activity.
- No autonomous external action is allowed.
- No external integrations are enabled.
- Candidate data is not used without active consent.
- Billing, payment, and money movement remain human-controlled.
