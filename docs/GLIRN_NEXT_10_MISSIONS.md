# GLIRN Next 10 Missions

## Objective

The next 10 missions should move GLIRN from internal readiness toward controlled pilot readiness without breaking the current safety model.

Current test status: 198 passed.

## Mission 49: Backup And Restore Readiness

Business purpose: Ensure Gareth can recover GLIRN before any real-world use.

Technical purpose: Document and test backup and restore procedures for code, data, logs, approvals, and docs.

Revenue relevance: Reduces operational risk before commercial use.

## Mission 50: Real-World Data Intake Specification

Business purpose: Define how candidate and client records can be entered without scraping or unsafe ingestion.

Technical purpose: Specify manual and CSV fields for candidates, clients, opportunities, consent, and terms.

Revenue relevance: Enables controlled movement from stub data to pilot data.

## Mission 51: Candidate Consent Evidence Pack

Business purpose: Create a defensible consent process before candidate use.

Technical purpose: Define consent evidence fields, expiry, status, source, and audit references.

Revenue relevance: Makes senior candidate workflows usable with lower trust risk.

## Mission 52: Client Terms Evidence Pack

Business purpose: Ensure no fee or candidate detail discussion proceeds without client terms readiness.

Technical purpose: Define client terms evidence, status, expiry, scope, and audit reference.

Revenue relevance: Supports fee proposals and invoice readiness.

## Mission 53: Manual Pilot Data Dry Run

Business purpose: Test GLIRN with a small controlled dataset before any live workflow.

Technical purpose: Load one client, one candidate, one opportunity, one consent record, and one terms record through a manual dry run.

Revenue relevance: Validates whether the platform can support a realistic placement workflow.

## Mission 54: Approval Evidence Bundle

Business purpose: Make Gareth approval easier and more reliable.

Technical purpose: Generate a single review bundle covering candidate, client, opportunity, consent, terms, compliance, revenue, and risk.

Revenue relevance: Speeds up human review while preserving control.

## Mission 55: Production Legal Wording Checklist

Business purpose: Prepare privacy, consent, terms, and outreach wording for professional review.

Technical purpose: Create checklists for public wording, candidate wording, client wording, privacy notice, and data retention.

Revenue relevance: Reduces friction before pilot outreach.

## Mission 56: Narrow Pilot Workflow Design

Business purpose: Define the first limited GLIRN pilot.

Technical purpose: Specify one practice area, one jurisdiction, one client type, one candidate type, one approval workflow.

Revenue relevance: Creates a realistic path to first commercial validation.

## Mission 57: Pilot Readiness Review

Business purpose: Decide whether GLIRN is ready to be tested with one controlled pilot.

Technical purpose: Review tests, dashboard status, backup status, consent evidence, client terms, approval workflow, and data minimisation.

Revenue relevance: Prevents premature launch.

## Mission 58: Release 1.2 Checkpoint

Business purpose: Freeze the post-pilot-readiness state.

Technical purpose: Document system status, tests, safety rules, gaps, and next operating decisions.

Revenue relevance: Creates a clean checkpoint before any external use.

## Mission Principles

- Documentation and readiness before integration.
- Manual data before live data.
- Consent and terms before outreach.
- Human approval before action.
- Backup and restore before pilot.
- No scraping.
- No autonomous outreach.
- No deployment actions without Gareth approval.
