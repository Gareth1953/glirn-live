# GLIRN First Revenue Blueprint

## Purpose

This document analyses the fastest realistic path from the current GLIRN platform to first revenue.

Business name: Global Legal Intelligence & Recruitment Network

Public-facing brand: David Sanson

Legal and tax owner: Gareth Price

Positioning: Human-led, technology-enhanced, Gareth-approved.

Current platform status: internal readiness platform with 198 passing tests.

## 1. Fastest Route To First Paying Client

The fastest route is not to sell GLIRN as software.

The fastest route is to sell a narrow legal intelligence and recruitment review to one clearly defined client type.

Recommended first offer:

"Senior Legal Hiring Intelligence Review for Private Equity and Corporate Legal Hiring."

What the client gets:

- A short legal hiring intelligence review.
- One priority role recommendation.
- One practice area demand view.
- One jurisdiction demand view.
- A fee and hiring difficulty estimate.
- A human-reviewed recommendation on whether a search is worth starting.

Why this is fastest:

- It does not require live platform access.
- It does not require integrations.
- It does not require the client to trust automation.
- It can be delivered manually with GLIRN supporting judgement internally.
- It creates a natural bridge to retained search or contingency placement.

Most realistic first paying client:

- Boutique law firm.
- Specialist legal practice.
- Smaller private equity-backed company.
- Founder-led legal services business.
- In-house legal team with an urgent senior hiring gap.

Likely first price:

- GBP 250 to GBP 750 for an intelligence review.
- Waive or credit the fee if the client agrees a search mandate.

## 2. Fastest Route To First Placement Fee

The fastest route to first placement fee is a narrow senior legal search where:

- The role is already needed.
- The client can make decisions quickly.
- Candidate consent can be handled manually.
- Fee terms can be agreed before candidate details are shared.
- The market is specialist enough that a targeted introduction has value.

Recommended route:

1. Start with a client intelligence conversation.
2. Identify one urgent senior legal hiring gap.
3. Agree fee terms before candidate details are shared.
4. Obtain candidate consent before introduction.
5. Use GLIRN internally to assess match quality, compliance readiness, and fee potential.

Most realistic first placement type:

- Senior Associate.
- Legal Director.
- Specialist Counsel.
- In-house Senior Legal Counsel.

Highest-value first placement type:

- Partner.
- General Counsel.
- Chief Legal Officer.

Fastest realistic placement fee is more likely from a mid-senior role than a full executive search, because the sales cycle may be shorter.

## 3. Recommended Target Legal Sector

Recommended first sector: Technology & AI Law.

Reasons:

- Clear demand growth.
- Clients understand the business pressure.
- Legal teams are still defining capability gaps.
- AI governance, contracting, data, privacy, IP, procurement, and risk overlap.
- Specialist lawyers are valuable.
- GLIRN's technology-enhanced positioning feels relevant.

Alternative high-value sector: Private Equity.

Reasons:

- Higher fee potential.
- Strong demand for commercial and transaction legal capability.
- Clear value from senior legal talent.

Pragmatic recommendation:

- Start with Technology & AI Law for first client conversations.
- Keep Private Equity as the higher-fee second lane.

## 4. Recommended Target Jurisdiction

Recommended first jurisdiction: England & Wales.

Reasons:

- Familiar legal market.
- Strong legal services ecosystem.
- Many law firms, boutiques, in-house legal teams, legal tech businesses, and private equity-backed companies.
- Easier to reason about compliance, consent, and client terms before expanding.

Secondary jurisdiction:

- United Arab Emirates.

Reasons:

- Active legal market.
- International legal hiring demand.
- Growing technology, infrastructure, energy, and corporate activity.

Recommendation:

Start with England & Wales. Do not expand jurisdictionally until the first workflow is proven.

## 5. Recommended Fee Structure

First paid service:

- Fixed-fee legal hiring intelligence review: GBP 250 to GBP 750.

First recruitment fee:

- Contingency placement fee: 20 percent to 25 percent of first-year compensation.

Executive search:

- 25 percent to 30 percent of first-year compensation.

Retained search:

- One-third retainer upfront.
- One-third at shortlist.
- One-third on placement.

Intelligence fee credit:

- If a client pays for an intelligence review and later signs a search mandate, the review fee can be credited against the final placement fee.

Important control:

No fee proposal should be sent without Gareth Price approval.

Why:

- Low capital.
- Suitable for David Sanson public brand.
- Works for legal market positioning.
- Does not require automation.
- Does not require scraping.
- Allows trust to build through visible expertise.

What to post:

- Short legal hiring intelligence observations.
- Practice area demand signals.
- Senior legal role prioritisation.
- Human-led recruitment positioning.

Avoid:

- Automation claims.

Secondary channel:

## 8. Top 10 Launch Risks

1. Trust risk: clients may not know David Sanson or GLIRN yet.
2. Compliance risk: candidate consent and client terms must be handled properly.
3. Data risk: GLIRN currently uses stub data only.
4. Positioning risk: market may confuse GLIRN with generic AI recruitment.
5. Sales risk: legal clients may not pay for intelligence unless the problem is urgent.
6. Execution risk: placement work requires human relationship management.
7. Evidence risk: market claims must be defensible and not overstated.
8. Fee risk: clients may resist recruitment fees without strong trust.
9. Operational risk: Gareth approval must be practical and timely.
10. Scope risk: expanding sectors or jurisdictions too early will dilute focus.

## 9. Top 10 Launch Opportunities

1. AI legal capability demand is rising.
2. Law firms need technology and AI law talent.
3. In-house teams need AI governance and commercial legal support.
4. Private equity-backed companies need stronger legal leadership.
5. Legal hiring is expensive, so better prioritisation has value.
6. Boutique firms may need specialist senior hires to grow.
7. Intelligence can open client conversations before recruitment selling.
8. Human-led positioning can reduce resistance to AI-enabled recruitment.
9. GLIRN's approval and compliance controls create differentiation.
10. A narrow first offer can generate revenue without full platform deployment.

## 10. 30-Day Launch Plan

### Days 1-3: Define The First Offer

- Finalise the GLIRN Senior Legal Hiring Intelligence Review.
- Set price at GBP 500.
- Define one-page deliverable structure.
- Choose first sector: Technology & AI Law.
- Choose first jurisdiction: England & Wales.

### Days 4-7: Prepare Market Positioning

- Publish LinkedIn profile for David Sanson.
- Prepare one-page GLIRN overview.
- Prepare a short sample intelligence review using non-private, non-scraped, general market reasoning.
- Prepare client call script.

### Days 8-12: Identify Warm Targets

- List 20 potential client profiles manually.
- Prioritise boutique law firms, legal tech companies, and growth companies with legal hiring pressure.
- Do not scrape.
- Do not automate outreach.

### Days 13-18: Human Outreach

- Send a small number of human-approved messages.
- Focus on asking whether legal hiring pressure exists.
- Do not claim candidate availability.
- Do not share candidate details.
- Offer the GBP 500 intelligence review.

### Days 19-22: Deliver First Review

- If one client agrees, deliver a narrow review.
- Use GLIRN internally for structure.
- Keep output concise.
- Make one recommendation: start search, monitor, defer, or reject.

### Days 23-26: Convert To Search Mandate

- If the client has a real hiring need, discuss terms.
- Record client terms before candidate details are shared.
- Require Gareth approval before fee proposal.

### Days 27-30: Review Results

- Did anyone pay?
- Did anyone show serious buying intent?
- Did the review create trust?
- Did the client want recruitment support?
- Was the sector right?
- Was the price acceptable?

Decision:

- If paid or strong intent: continue.
- If interest but no payment: refine offer.
- If no interest: pivot sector, buyer, or offer.

## Recommended First Move

Do not try to sell the full GLIRN platform.

Sell one paid GLIRN Senior Legal Hiring Intelligence Review for Technology & AI Law in England & Wales.

Use that review to create the first client conversation, first payment, and potentially the first search mandate.
