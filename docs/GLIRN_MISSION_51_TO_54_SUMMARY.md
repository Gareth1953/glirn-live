# GLIRN Mission 51 To 54 Summary

## Overview

Missions 51 to 54 transformed GLIRN from an internal intelligence and recruitment control system into a draft-generation and revenue-command system.

The platform remains read-only for commercial action, human-controlled, and non-autonomous.

Current test status: 226 passed.

## Mission 51: Automated Intelligence Review Engine

Business purpose: Give Gareth a draft client-facing intelligence review without manually assembling data from every GLIRN module.

Technical purpose: Generate a structured GLIRN Senior Legal Hiring Intelligence Review from internal GLIRN data.

Output:

- Executive Summary.
- Client Context.
- Practice Area Focus.
- Jurisdiction Focus.
- Market Signal Summary.
- Hiring Difficulty Assessment.
- Recommended Priority Role.
- Candidate Profile Specification.
- Indicative Fee Model.
- Compliance Summary.
- Recommended Action.

Safety:

- Draft generation only.
- No automatic client delivery.
- No candidate personal data without active consent.
- Client-ready status requires Gareth approval.

## Mission 52: Client Deliverable Factory

Business purpose: Convert GLIRN intelligence into practical commercial documents Gareth can review and use manually.

Technical purpose: Generate draft deliverables from Executive Autopilot, Intelligence Review Engine, Client Acquisition, Candidate Discovery, Matching, Commercial Revenue, and Compliance Core.

Output:

- Search Mandate Proposal.
- Executive Search Proposal.
- Fee Proposal.
- Candidate Shortlist Report.
- Market Intelligence Report.
- Client Meeting Brief.

Safety:

- Draft generation only.
- No autonomous delivery.
- No autonomous fee proposals.
- No autonomous contracts.
- Gareth approval required before client-ready status.

## Mission 53: Approval-to-Action Workflow

Business purpose: Prevent generated drafts from being mistaken for approved client-ready materials.

Technical purpose: Create a controlled workflow from draft to Gareth approval to human-use readiness.

Output:

- Draft status.
- Approval status.
- Client-ready status.
- Action readiness status.
- Pending Gareth approval queue.
- Approved for human use queue.
- Rejected items.
- Monitored items.

Safety:

- Nothing becomes client-ready without Gareth approval.
- No automatic delivery.
- No outreach.
- No external connections.
- Every approval action audit logged.

## Mission 54: Revenue Command Centre

Business purpose: Show Gareth where the money is, what is closest to revenue, and what should be reviewed first.

Technical purpose: Aggregate revenue-relevant data from Opportunity Radar, Executive Autopilot, Matching Engine, Commercial Revenue Engine, Client Deliverable Factory, Approval-to-Action Workflow, and Daily Executive Briefing.

Output:

- Total Revenue Pipeline.
- Estimated Placement Fee Pipeline.
- Estimated Intelligence Review Revenue.
- Approved Opportunities Count.
- Approved Deliverables Count.
- Highest Fee Opportunity.
- Fastest Revenue Opportunity.
- Revenue Readiness Score.
- Revenue Funnel.
- Top Revenue Opportunities.
- Top Quick Win Opportunities.
- Highest Probability Revenue.
- Dave Recommends First.

Safety:

- Read-only.
- No outreach.
- No client delivery.
- No fee proposals.
- No contracts.
- No invoicing.
- No external integrations.
- No scraping.
- No live data fetching.
- Human approval mandatory.

## Combined Platform Impact

Missions 51 to 54 added the commercial operating layer:

- Draft intelligence can be generated.
- Draft deliverables can be generated.
- Drafts remain controlled until approval.
- Revenue path can be viewed in one place.
- Gareth can see what is valuable, what is closest to revenue, and what is blocked.

## Remaining Gap

The platform can now prepare and rank revenue material, but it has not yet validated a real first-client workflow.

Recommended next action: define the first-client readiness gate before any external use.
