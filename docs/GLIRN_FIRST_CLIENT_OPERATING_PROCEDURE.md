# GLIRN First Client Operating Procedure

## Purpose

This procedure defines how Gareth should use GLIRN when the platform identifies a real first-client opportunity.

GLIRN may assess, rank, generate drafts, detect blockers, and recommend next actions. GLIRN must not contact clients, contact candidates, send deliverables, issue fee proposals, accept contracts, invoice, publish, scrape, fetch live data, or connect to external services.

Human approval remains mandatory.

## 1. Client Terms Procedure

### When Terms Are Required

Client terms are required before:

- Candidate details are shared.
- A search mandate is discussed as a commercial engagement.
- A fee proposal is used externally.
- A placement process begins.
- An invoice can be considered ready.

Client terms should be recorded before any candidate-specific or fee-specific client activity.

### Approval Requirements

Gareth must approve:

- Any proposed client terms.
- Any change to fee structure.
- Any search mandate discussion.
- Any client-facing fee proposal.
- Any movement from intelligence review to recruitment mandate.

GLIRN may identify missing terms and block readiness, but it must not negotiate, accept, or issue terms automatically.

### Record Keeping

Record:

- Client name.
- Client contact route.
- Terms status: missing, proposed, recorded, expired, or rejected.
- Date terms were recorded.
- Gareth approval status.
- Any restrictions on use.
- Audit reference for the approval decision.

## 2. Payment Procedure

### Review Fee Process

For a paid intelligence review:

1. Confirm the review scope.
2. Confirm the price.
3. Confirm whether candidate-specific information is excluded.
4. Confirm client terms.
5. Confirm payment route.
6. Obtain Gareth approval.
7. Mark the review as ready for human delivery only.

GLIRN may generate the review draft and readiness assessment. Gareth handles all real-world payment and delivery steps.

### Search Fee Process

For a recruitment search fee:

1. Confirm search mandate scope.
2. Confirm fee type: contingency, retained, executive search, or agreed hybrid.
3. Confirm candidate consent requirements.
4. Confirm client terms.
5. Confirm Gareth approval before any client-facing fee discussion.
6. Record the fee model and approval decision.

GLIRN may estimate fees and recommend a fee model. It must not issue a fee proposal automatically.

### Invoice Readiness Process

Invoice readiness requires:

- Recorded client terms.
- Confirmed fee model.
- Confirmed payment process.
- Completed human approval.
- Delivery or placement event verified by Gareth.
- No active compliance blocker.

GLIRN may flag invoice readiness. It must not issue invoices.

## 3. Manual Delivery Procedure

### Approved Deliverable Handling

Before using any generated deliverable:

1. Confirm it appears in the approved-for-human-use queue.
2. Confirm Gareth approval is recorded.
3. Confirm client terms status if client-facing.
4. Confirm candidate consent if candidate-specific.
5. Check that no prohibited personal data appears.
6. Check that fee language is approved.
7. Save the final human-reviewed version.

Generated deliverables are drafts until Gareth approval is recorded.

### Approved Review Handling

Before using a generated intelligence review:

1. Confirm the review is approved for human-controlled use.
2. Confirm candidate personal data is excluded unless active consent exists.
3. Confirm jurisdiction and compliance notes are reviewed.
4. Confirm the recommended action is suitable.
5. Confirm the client-facing version is manually checked.

### Approval Checks Before Use

Every external-use item must pass:

- Approval-to-Action Workflow.
- First Client Readiness Gate.
- Launch Readiness Command Centre.
- Gareth approval.

If any one of these is blocked, the item is not ready for human use.

## 4. Compliance Procedure

### Consent Checks

Check whether candidate consent is:

- Active.
- Missing.
- Expired.
- Restricted.
- Not required because the material is anonymised and non-candidate-specific.

Candidate-specific material requires active consent.

### Jurisdiction Checks

Before first-client use, confirm:

- Client jurisdiction.
- Candidate jurisdiction, if relevant.
- Applicable recruitment compliance considerations.
- Any data protection restrictions.
- Whether external review is needed.

GLIRN may flag jurisdiction and readiness issues. Gareth must decide whether professional compliance review is required.

### Approval Checks

Gareth approval is required before:

- Any client contact.
- Any candidate contact.
- Any candidate introduction.
- Any candidate detail sharing.
- Any fee proposal.
- Any search mandate.
- Any client-facing report.
- Any invoice-ready status.

## 5. Candidate Consent Procedure

### When Candidate Consent Is Required

Candidate consent is required before:

- Candidate identity is shared.
- Candidate details are sent to a client.
- Candidate-specific intelligence is included.
- Candidate is introduced.
- Candidate is discussed in a client-facing placement context.

Consent is not optional for candidate-specific external use.

### Validation Process

Validate:

- Candidate ID.
- Consent status.
- Consent scope.
- Consent date.
- Consent expiry.
- Jurisdiction.
- Restrictions.
- Gareth approval status.

If consent is missing, expired, restricted, or unclear, the item is blocked.

### Audit Process

Record:

- Consent check result.
- Date checked.
- Source of consent record.
- Gareth approval decision.
- Any blocker or restriction.
- Whether candidate details were excluded or anonymised.

## 6. First Prospect Procedure

### Selecting Prospect

Select one prospect only.

The first prospect should be:

- A law firm, boutique practice, legal department, or legal-sector buyer.
- In a clearly defined jurisdiction.
- Matched to a clear practice area.
- Suitable for a narrow intelligence-led conversation.
- Not dependent on candidate personal data.
- Not dependent on scraping or private data.

### Readiness Review

Before action:

1. Review the Revenue Command Centre.
2. Review the First Client Readiness Gate.
3. Review the Launch Readiness Command Centre.
4. Confirm missing items.
5. Confirm blocked items.
6. Confirm recommended next action.

### Approval Workflow

If the item is suitable:

1. Gareth approves the item for human planning.
2. Gareth approves any human delivery step separately.
3. Gareth records the approval reason.
4. GLIRN audit logging records the decision.

Approval does not permit autonomous action.

## 7. First Client Workflow

Opportunity -> Review Generated -> Readiness Checked -> Gareth Approval -> Human Delivery -> Client Response -> Search Mandate -> Placement -> Invoice

### Workflow Detail

1. Opportunity

GLIRN identifies and ranks the opportunity.

2. Review Generated

GLIRN generates a draft intelligence review.

3. Readiness Checked

GLIRN runs first-client and launch readiness checks.

4. Gareth Approval

Gareth approves, rejects, monitors, or resets to planning.

5. Human Delivery

If approved, Gareth manually delivers or discusses the material.

6. Client Response

Gareth records whether the client is interested, neutral, or not interested.

7. Search Mandate

If the client wants recruitment support, Gareth confirms terms, fee model, consent requirements, and mandate scope.

8. Placement

Candidate activity begins only after consent and approval controls pass.

9. Invoice

Invoice readiness is assessed only after terms, fee model, delivery or placement event, and Gareth approval are complete.

## 8. First GBP 500 Workflow

Objective: earn a small intelligence review fee before attempting a placement.

Steps:

1. Select one prospect profile.
2. Generate a draft intelligence review.
3. Keep candidate personal data excluded.
4. Run First Client Readiness Gate.
5. Run Launch Readiness Command Centre.
6. Confirm client terms and payment process.
7. Gareth approves human delivery.
8. Gareth manually presents the intelligence review offer.
9. If accepted, Gareth handles payment manually.
10. Gareth records outcome and lessons.

Rules:

- No automatic delivery.
- No automatic payment collection.
- No candidate details.
- No fee proposal without Gareth approval.

## 9. First Placement Fee Workflow

Objective: convert intelligence interest into a search mandate and then a placement fee.

Steps:

1. Identify client hiring need.
2. Confirm search mandate scope.
3. Confirm client terms.
4. Confirm fee model.
5. Confirm candidate consent process.
6. Gareth approves search mandate discussion.
7. Candidate discovery remains consent-gated.
8. Candidate matching remains approval-gated.
9. Candidate details are shared only with active consent and Gareth approval.
10. Placement outcome is recorded.
11. Invoice readiness is assessed.
12. Gareth handles invoicing manually.

Rules:

- No candidate introduction without approval.
- No client-facing candidate details without active consent.
- No placement action without Gareth approval.
- No invoicing by GLIRN.

## 10. Gareth Approval Matrix

### What GLIRN May Do Automatically

- Rank opportunities.
- Generate draft intelligence reviews.
- Generate draft deliverables.
- Estimate revenue potential.
- Estimate readiness scores.
- Detect missing consent.
- Detect missing client terms.
- Detect missing payment process.
- Detect missing manual delivery process.
- Recommend next action.
- Block unsafe items.
- Log approval decisions.

### What Gareth Must Approve

- First-client action.
- External use of any review.
- External use of any deliverable.
- Client contact.
- Candidate contact.
- Candidate introduction.
- Candidate detail sharing.
- Search mandate discussion.
- Fee proposal.
- Client terms acceptance.
- Payment process use.
- Invoice readiness.
- Any launch activity.

### What GLIRN Must Never Do

- Launch automatically.
- Publish a website.
- Post to LinkedIn.
- Contact clients.
- Contact candidates.
- Send deliverables.
- Issue fee proposals.
- Accept contracts.
- Issue invoices.
- Move capital.
- Trade.
- Scrape websites.
- Fetch live data.
- Connect to external systems without approval.
- Make automated recruitment decisions.

## Operating Rule

If there is uncertainty, missing consent, missing client terms, missing payment process, missing compliance review, or missing Gareth approval, the item is blocked.
