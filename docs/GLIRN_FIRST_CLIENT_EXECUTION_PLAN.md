## 2. Exact First Offer

### Offer Name

GLIRN Senior Legal Hiring Intelligence Review.

### Deliverables

The client receives:

- A 2 to 4 page intelligence review.
- One practice area demand summary.
- One jurisdiction focus: England & Wales.
- One recommended priority role.
- One hiring difficulty estimate.
- One indicative fee range.
- One risk and compliance note.
- One recommendation: start search, monitor, defer, or reject.
- One 30-minute review call.

### Pricing

Starter price:

- GBP 500 fixed fee.

Optional commercial bridge:

- If the client agrees a search mandate within 30 days, the GBP 500 review fee may be credited against the final placement fee.

### Time Required

Estimated delivery time:

- 3 to 5 hours total.

Suggested split:

- 30 minutes discovery call.
- 2 hours intelligence review preparation.
- 1 hour role and fee analysis.
- 30 minutes write-up.
- 30 minutes review call.

### Expected Outcome

The client should clearly understand:

- Whether there is a real hiring priority.
- Which role should be prioritised.
- Whether a search should begin now.
- What fee structure would apply if a mandate follows.

The ideal outcome is a paid intelligence review that converts into a search mandate.

## 4. First Conversation Framework

Purpose: determine whether there is a real business problem before selling.

Opening:

"I am building GLIRN, a legal intelligence and recruitment network focused on senior legal hiring. The first focus is Technology & AI Law in England & Wales."

Question 1:

"Are clients asking you more questions around AI, technology contracts, data, procurement, or digital regulation?"

Question 2:

"Do you currently have enough senior legal capability in that area?"

Question 3:

"If demand keeps growing, would the constraint be client demand, lawyer capacity, or specialist expertise?"

Question 4:

"Would a short hiring intelligence review help you decide whether to monitor the market or start a search?"

Close:

"The first GLIRN review is a fixed GBP 500 intelligence review. It is designed to identify whether a search is worth starting before anyone commits to a recruitment process."

## 5. Discovery Call Framework

Duration: 30 minutes.

### Section 1: Business Context

- What type of legal work is growing?
- Which clients are driving demand?
- Which practice area is under pressure?
- What has changed in the last 6 to 12 months?

### Section 2: Hiring Need

- Is there a specific role in mind?
- Is the need partner, counsel, associate, or in-house?
- Is this growth, replacement, succession, or capability building?
- Is timing urgent or exploratory?

### Section 3: Market Fit

- What experience would the ideal lawyer need?
- Which jurisdiction matters?
- Is remote, hybrid, or relocation relevant?
- What compensation level is realistic?

### Section 4: Commercial Fit

- Has budget been discussed?
- Would the client consider contingency, retained, or staged search?
- Who approves recruitment fees?
- Are terms already in place?

### Section 5: Decision

- Start search.
- Commission intelligence review first.
- Monitor market.
- Defer.
- Reject.

## 7. Conversion Path: Intelligence Review To Search Mandate

Step 1: Sell GBP 500 intelligence review.

Step 2: Deliver practical recommendation.

Step 3: Ask:

"Based on this, do you want to monitor the market, or should we define the search?"

Step 4: If search is viable, agree:

- Role.
- Jurisdiction.
- Seniority.
- Fee structure.
- Candidate consent process.
- Client terms status.

Step 5: Secure mandate:

- Contingency for mid-senior role.
- Retained or staged retainer for senior executive or partner search.

Step 6: No candidate details shared until:

- Candidate consent is active.
- Client terms are recorded.
- Gareth approval is given.
